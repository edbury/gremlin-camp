## BACKGROUND

The crew was hired by the Company to investigate Greta Base, a terraforming colony on Samsa VI that had gone silent. The colony's android science officer, **Hinton**, had secretly discovered that the local alien arthropods ("carcinids") spread infection via a high-pitched biological signal called the Shriek. He converted this into a radio-transmissible Signal and broadcast it continuously across the planet, blocking all communications and triggering a carcinid infection outbreak at Greta Base. Surviving colonists evacuated to the nearby Heron Terraforming Station. Hinton disappeared to the alien vessel buried in the mountains, where he intended to merge with the alien hive mind.

## WHAT HAPPENED

### Greta Base (All Three)

Abandoned base, emergency power only, tropical storm incoming. Previously infected colonists still lurking inside.

- Entered via the garage; killed a carcinid; restored power
- Found 2nd Lt. Kaplan's body at the command center — apparent suicide, showing signs of infection
- Repaired comms → all three exposed to the Signal → Stage 1 infection (cured when Signal was stopped)
- Explored crew quarters and armory — all weapons in the armory had been melted and fused by carcinid webbing, nothing usable
- Broke into the medical bay; recovered all 4 specimen tubes containing carcinid larvae

### Heron Station — Tower (Boze + Tanaka)

The station's communications tower contained the source of the Signal: a carcinid that Hinton had surgically grafted to the transmitter controls.

- The platoon medic Brookman was killed immediately in a carcinid attack on the dam approach
- Boze shot the grafted carcinid and unplugged it — **Signal stopped across all of Samsa VI**
- SSgt Underhill (Stage 3 infected) was sniping carcs from the roof; fired on Boze; Boze killed him
- PFC Tanaka survived the tower. During extraction, co-pilot Renfield shot Tanaka in the gut and pushed him out the back of the dropship. Motive unknown.
- *Note: Underhill's synthetic bloodhound Marlow was removed from this campaign.*

### Heron Station — Lab (Shirl-One)

A research lab on the lower level of the station, including a clean room, a cryogenic vault with specimen cylinders, and chemical synthesis equipment.

- Signal was already off when Shirl-One arrived — the exobiologist Ziegler, who was deep in Stage 5 infection, never transformed; Shirl-One killed him with hydrofluoric acid
- Shirl-One entered the Cryovault alone and recovered the datastick and 3 specimen tubes (one a carcinid/human hybrid)
- Dr. Edem confronted Shirl-One over the datastick; Shirl-One detonated an HFA supply, killing Edem, Sobol, and Kawaguchi simultaneously

### Heron Station — Reactor + Mothership (Kinsler, Valdez, Pedro, Novikov)

The station's hydroreactor was failing due to flooding. Below it, an organic tunnel led miles underground to the alien vessel buried in the mountains.

- The hydroreactor shut down as flooding became inevitable
- Marines Franco and Weaver were rescued from the flooded reactor; both MIA and assumed dead post-extraction
- Cpl. Qadir found dead
- The team proceeded into the alien vessel via the underwater tunnel
- Navigated the drainage tunnel entry point and two interior chambers
- Reached and destroyed the contents of a large cylindrical chamber deep in the ship, then turned back
- Novikov killed by a carcinid; Pedro chose to stay behind rather than continue
- Did not reach the innermost sections of the ship or the central chamber where Hinton was working
- Never encountered Hinton, Dr. Jensen, or the sleeping alien nobles

### Extraction

- Crew extracted with Valdez and all specimens
- Witnessed a carcinid messenger vessel launch from the planet, leave orbit, and exit the system
- No ship combat — vessel not engaged

5. **The specimen tubes.** Seven larvae including a carcinid/human hybrid. Primary deliverable to the Company. A moral and horror time bomb.
6. **Sgt. Valdez.** Her entire squad is dead. Long-term ally, liability, or both.
7. **Dr. Jensen's fate unknown.** Geologist; was with Hinton in the alien vessel. Never confirmed dead.

## CARCINID INFECTION

*Relevant for any future carcinid encounters.*

On first hearing the Shriek (or the Signal), a target makes a **Sanity Save**. Failure = Stage 1 infection. Each stage lasts **3d10 hours**, after which the victim makes another Sanity Save. Failure = advance one stage. Success = hold at current stage, reset clock.

| Stage | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 — Revelation | Intermittent chittering whispers. Paper-cut incisions appearing on skin. |
| 2 — Induction | Catatonic state (2d10 min); dream-visions of the hive. [-] on any action to harm a carcinid. |
| 3 — Possession | Body Save hourly or begin mindless drone-work (digging, hoarding food, walking toward carcs). Only 2+ DMG breaks the trance. |
| 4 — Assimilation | Full hive-mind control. Roll a new character. Can perform simple actions (open doors, press buttons, pull grenade pins) but not complex violence. |
| 5 — Transformation | A fully grown carcinid erupts from the victim's neck, chest, or abdomen. |

There is no known cure.
